What Is a Laser Frenectomy?

A Laser Frenectomy is a medical procedure that involves the removal or modification of a frenulum, a small tissue band that connects the lip, tongue, or cheek to the gums. This tissue can sometimes cause restrictions, leading to difficulty with speech, eating, or proper oral development. In many cases, a frenulum that is too tight can affect babies, children, and adults in various ways, including challenges in breastfeeding, speech development, or oral hygiene.

By using laser technology, our experienced team in Richmond can perform this procedure in a way that is less invasive, requires no stitches, and promotes a quicker recovery. The laser not only removes the excess tissue but also cauterizes the area, reducing bleeding and discomfort.

Traditional frenectomy procedures often involve using a scalpel, which may lead to more discomfort, a longer healing time, and an increased risk of infection. However, with Laser Frenectomy in Richmond, you can expect several advantages that will improve your overall experience and recovery:

One of the key benefits of laser frenectomy is the minimal discomfort it offers. The procedure uses advanced technology that results in less pain and allows for a more comfortable recovery compared to traditional methods. The laser cauterizes the tissue as it cuts, reducing bleeding, and there is no need for stitches, which can sometimes cause further irritation. As a result, recovery times are significantly shorter, allowing you to get back to your normal routine in no time.

Laser Frenectomy in Richmond: How It Works

When considering Laser Frenectomy in Richmond, you may wonder what the process involves. Here’s an overview of how the procedure works:

  • Consultation: The dentist evaluates the condition of the frenulum and discusses concerns.
  • Local Anesthesia: The area is numbed to ensure comfort during the procedure.
  • Laser Treatment: The laser is used to precisely remove or reshape the frenulum, cauterizing the tissue as it works.
  • Aftercare: Detailed instructions are given for a smooth recovery.

What to Expect After a Laser Frenectomy

The recovery period following a laser frenectomy is usually brief, with minimal discomfort. Most patients recover in 24-48 hours. Mild swelling or tenderness may be felt, but it subsides within a few days. Over-the-counter pain relievers are usually recommended for comfort.
